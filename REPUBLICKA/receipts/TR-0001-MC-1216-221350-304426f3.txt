Reconciliation Day is a public holiday in the ACT, first held on Monday 28 May 2018, making it the first Australian jurisdiction to gazette a Reconciliation‑focused holiday.​

Key facts in one sentence
It marks the start of National Reconciliation Week, is held on the first Monday on or after 27 May each year, and commemorates the 1967 referendum and broader truth‑telling and healing with Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples.​



WHY IS IT ILLEGAL?



Reconciliation Day itself is not illegal; it is a lawful public holiday created by the ACT government.​

How it was made lawful
The ACT Legislative Assembly passed amendments to the Holidays Act to establish Reconciliation Day as an official territory public holiday, starting in 2018, which is exactly how other public holidays are created. Once gazetted, employers, schools, and services in the ACT must observe it under standard employment and holiday law, just like ANZAC Day or Labour Day.​
