A Devil's Contract, or Faustian bargain, is a legendary pact where a human trades their soul (or something valuable) to the Devil or a demon for worldly goods, knowledge, power, or favors, often leading to eternal damnation. In modern fiction, like Chainsaw Man, contracts involve exchanging specific things (body parts, lifespan, senses) for powers, with devils needing to honor the binding terms, though loopholes and suffering are common elements, notes The Evil Wiki and Reddit users.
Core Concepts
The Exchange: A human offers something precious (soul, body parts, lifespan, loved ones).
The Reward: In return, the Devil grants supernatural abilities, wealth, fame, knowledge, or protection.
The Price: The cost is often paid incrementally or when the power is used, causing suffering or loss.
The Binding: The contract is a powerful, unbreakable bond; devils cannot lie or renege on the terms.
In Folklore & Religion
Faust Legend: The classic tale of Dr. Faust, who sold his soul to the demon Mephistopheles for limitless knowledge.
Witchcraft: Traditional beliefs linked pacts with demons to witchcraft, granting powers in exchange for servitude.

Loopholes & Traps: Contracts are complex, often containing hidden clauses or loopholes that benefit the Devil, notes Reddit users.
Metaphorical Meaning
